Sinigang na Isda with Hipon Recipe

Sinigang na Isda with Hipon is a traditional Filipino sour soup made with fresh fish and shrimp, simmered with vegetables in a tangy tamarind broth. This comforting dish is perfect for rainy days and pairs wonderfully with steamed rice. Serves 4–5 people and takes around 45 minutes to cook.

Ingredients

  • 500g fish (tilapia, bangus, or any white fish), cleaned and sliced
  • 250g shrimp (hipon), cleaned and deveined
  • 8 cups water
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 long green chilies (siling haba)
  • 1 radish (labanos), peeled and sliced
  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach) leaves
  • 1 pack sinigang mix (tamarind soup base) or fresh tamarind if available
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (patis)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, pour the water and bring it to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Add the tomatoes and onion. Let it simmer for about 5 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the radish, eggplant, and green chilies. Continue cooking for another 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the sinigang mix (or fresh tamarind juice) and fish sauce. Mix well to dissolve.
  5. Add the fish pieces and simmer gently for 5–7 minutes until the fish is cooked through.
  6. Add the shrimp and cook until they turn pink, about 3–4 minutes.
  7. Put in the string beans and kangkong leaves. Cook for another 2 minutes or until the vegetables are tender but not overcooked.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or more fish sauce as needed.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy your hearty sinigang!

Tip: For extra flavor, use fresh tamarind or add a few drops of calamansi juice before serving!
